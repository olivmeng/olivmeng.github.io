Ice-Ocean Interactions

Projections of sea level rise by 2100 can vary by 850~mm depending on the rate of iceberg calving at Greenland and Antarctic ice sheet margins (Pfeffer et al., 2008). Calving laws used in current ice-sheet models predict calving rates using empirically tuned strain rate or stress criteria, which is inadequate to capture the complex external interactions that modulate calving and are strongly coupled with the warming climate. In particular, how calving depends on ice-ocean interactions is poorly understood. Our group will leverage modeling, cold room laboratory, and remote observation capabilities to unravel the physics of ice-ocean interactions, which has crucial scientific, civil, and military implications.

We use poromechanics to investigate whether water-filled crevasses in ice slabs can propagate vertically through an underlying porous firn layer.
